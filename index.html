Important — Read This First
Your quiz setup is saved only on your computer (in your browser's local storage). If you send someone the link to this page, they will not see your categories or questions — they'll get a blank quiz.

To share your quiz with someone else:
1. Go to the ⚙ Setup tab
2. Scroll down to Import / Export
3. Click ↓ Export JSON — this downloads a small file containing your entire quiz
4. Send that file to the other person (email, Slack, USB stick — whatever works)
5. They open this page, go to ⚙ Setup, click ↑ Import JSON, and pick the file you sent

That's it — they'll now have an exact copy of your quiz on their computer.
What Is This?
Musical Boogaloo is a music quiz game. You set up categories (like "Classic Rock" or "90s Pop"), add YouTube clips as questions at different point levels (30, 40, and 50 points), and then play them during a quiz night. Think of it like a Jeopardy-style board — contestants pick a category and a point value, you play the clip, and they try to guess the song or artist.
Step-by-Step Setup
Step 1 — Name your event
Go to ⚙ Setup and type a name at the top (e.g. "Friday Night Quiz"). This is just for display — call it whatever you like.

Step 2 — Create categories
Type a category name (e.g. "Classic Rock") in the box under "Add New Category" and click + Add Category. You can add up to 40 categories. Each category gets 3 question slots: 30 points (easiest), 40 points (medium), and 50 points (hardest).

Step 3 — Add questions
Click the expand arrow on a category to see its question slots. Click + Add Question on any slot. You'll need:
  • A YouTube URL (or just the video ID) for the song
  • A start time and end time (in M:SS format, e.g. 1:30) — this is the clip that plays
  • The artist name — this is the answer that gets revealed
  • Optionally, a song title (shown after the answer is revealed)

Step 4 — Reorder categories
Drag categories up and down in the setup list to change the order they appear on the soundboard.

Step 5 — You're ready!
Switch to the ▶ Soundboard tab. You'll see all your categories in a grid. Each circle is a question — click it to open the game modal, play the clip, and reveal the answer when ready.
During the Game
Playing a question: Click any point circle on the soundboard. A popup will appear with play/stop controls. Hit ▶ Play to start the clip. The music plays for the duration you set (start → end time).

Revealing the answer: When the contestants have guessed (or given up), click Reveal Answer. The artist and song title will appear. The circle turns green to show it's been used.

Resetting the board: If you want to play another round with the same questions, click ↺ Reset Game at the top of the soundboard. This clears all the green "used" markers but keeps your questions intact.
Tips
• Pick recognisable parts of songs — a famous chorus or riff works better than a random verse.
• Keep clips short — 15 to 30 seconds is usually enough.
• Test your clips beforehand — make sure each one plays correctly before quiz night.
• Back up your quiz — use Export JSON to save a copy. If you clear your browser data, your quiz is gone.
• 30p = easy, 50p = hard — put well-known hits at 30 points and obscure tracks at 50.
